Title Description
The Swiss Family Robinson The Swiss Family Robinson is a classic adventure novel by Johann David Wyss, first published in 1812. It tells the story of a Swiss family shipwrecked on a deserted tropical island. Together, they use their resourcefulness and creativity to survive, building a new life filled with exploration, ingenuity, and adventure. The novel emphasizes themes of self-reliance, teamwork, and the wonders of nature.
The Gorgon's Head The Gorgon's Head is a classic mythological tale that recounts the hero Perseus's quest to defeat Medusa, one of the three Gorgons whose gaze turns people to stone. Armed with divine gifts and courage, Perseus embarks on a perilous journey to slay Medusa, using her severed head as a powerful weapon. This story is a timeless adventure, blending heroism, fate, and the triumph of good over evil in ancient Greek mythology
Harry Potter And The Philosopher's Stone Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone follows the story of an orphaned boy, Harry, who discovers on his 11th birthday that he is a wizard. He receives an invitation to attend Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, where he learns about magic, makes new friends, and uncovers the truth about his parents' mysterious deaths. Along the way, Harry faces dark forces as he confronts the villainous Voldemort, who seeks the powerful Philosopher's Stone to regain his lost power.
Alice's Adventures in Wonderland Alice's Adventures in Wonderland is a classic novel by Lewis Carroll, where a young girl named Alice falls down a rabbit hole into a fantastical world filled with strange creatures, perplexing puzzles, and whimsical adventures. In this dreamlike land, she encounters eccentric characters like the Cheshire Cat, the Mad Hatter, and the Queen of Hearts, each with their own bizarre logic, as she tries to find her way back home. The story is a blend of surrealism, imagination, and playful absurdity, captivating readers of all ages.
The Merchant of Venice The Merchant of Venice is a play by William Shakespeare that explores themes of justice, mercy, and revenge. Set in Venice, the story revolves around a wealthy merchant, Antonio, who borrows money from the Jewish moneylender Shylock to help his friend Bassanio court the heiress Portia. When Antonio is unable to repay the loan, Shylock demands a pound of his flesh as collateral. The play culminates in a tense courtroom drama, where themes of prejudice, love, and forgiveness unfold.
